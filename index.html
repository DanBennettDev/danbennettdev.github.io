Daniel Bennett

I am a phd student in human computer interaction, in the Bristol Interaction Group at the University of Bristol. My research focuses on issues of human autonomy in interaction: how intentions are formed and altered during interaction. I am also a musician, and interested in tools for musical creativity.

I have degrees in philosophy and computer science, and I have an interest in cognitive psychology. These three things are a good map of the corners of my current research.

Previously I worked as the ‘Information and Performance Systems Manager’ for a large NHS trust - a mixture of database management, BI development & IT strategy.

I have pages on twitter, github, google scholar and I have a newsletter, and an email address
